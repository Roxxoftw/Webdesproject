Healer Guide

Welcome to the Healer guide!

This guide will help you learn all the things that you need to know
so that you can become a reliable medic for your party members.

Emnity

Emnity, also known as threat or hate, is a mechanic that causes the enemy monsters to target and attack a player.
When multiple players are attacking an enemy, enemy A.I. will target the player with the highest enmity.
Players increase their enmity by attacking, healing or performing specific enmity increasing actions.
Basically Emnity how much attention enemies are giving you.

Like the tank, you should know about what skills you have that generate
emnity so you can avoid getting unwanted attention.

If your tank is half decent you dont need to worry about this anyway.


Emnity Bar

The Emnity Bar will be one of your closest friends.

The bar will fill up as you attract enemy attention. When your emnity bar has the letter A on it, that means you are the target!
You will need to maintain this emnity level though, as healers' abilities also generate emnity such as the White Mage's Cure.

Again, If your tank is half decent you dont need to worry about this anyway.


Being a field monitor

As a support class, being the monitor for ally hp, status and conditions happens often.
You will be the one who keeps the party train running. Without you, the
party will fall apart.



Keeping your team alive

Healing is your job, as the name suggests, you need to heal your party to actually complete a dungeon run!
Now with the obvious aside lets' look at what skills you'll be using for healing

First of all, there are three classes. White mage, Scholar and Astrologian





Classes

White mages focus on pure healing.
They have the most potent heals.
Straight Forward and easy to play.

Astrologians are like a hybrid of
the Scholar and White mage.
They have shields and heals.
Their sellings points are their cards
That give buffs to parties.

The Scholar focuses on DoTs, AoEs
Barriers and shields.
They have summons that help
heal in combat.


Healing

Healing is your primary job, but remember you don't need to heal a player when they receive 5% HP damage per 5 seconds.

As much as possible,

try to avoid overhealing someone. Don't heal someone at 90% HP when you know that your heal can give them 30% of their HP back.
Healing generates emnity and you have to keep your emnity as low as possible. Always focus on the tank, the tank is the priority
because when the tank falls, your party will most certainly follow.

AoE skills

Most of the time, you would only need to cast these skills when there is a party-wide AoE.
Focus is needed because these skills reduce the damage the party takes.

Here we have an example of an AoE attack from
the enemy wherein the healer counteracts with
Succor, an AoE heal and shield generator.
Sacred soil is also shown, which reduces 10% of
incoming damage no matter where it is from.

Other healers have similar abilities, for the AST its the Collective Unconcious, for the
White Mage it's Asylum which grants regen.

Emnity Management

There are somethings that you must consider when healing:

1.Don't overheal, heal when you must, not when you can.
2.Be able to predict damage so you can pre-cast heals.
3.Use role skills to lower emnity when needed.

Role Skills


Lucid
Dreaming

Esuna

Protect

These are the 3 essential Role skills that a healer should have.

Lucid Dreaming cuts current emnity down by half.
Esuna cures status effects.
Protect is a buff that reduces both physical and magic damage received

Healing Priority

1. Tanks
2. Yourself/Other healers
3.DPS

Other Notes

Healer is a relatively easy job to play early on but becomes evidently important on higher tier dungeons
and raids. Learning this class will take time, it is an easy job to use but hard to master.
Each healer has an emergency heal of some sort. Use these with care as they have
long cooldowns and using it without much effect would be a gigantic waste.
