Tank Guide

Welcome to the tank guide!
This guide will help you learn all the things that you need to know
so that you can become a reliable shield for your party members.

Emnity

Emnity, also known as threat or hate, is a mechanic that causes the enemy monsters to target and attack a player.
When multiple players are attacking an enemy, enemy A.I. will target the player with the highest enmity.
Players increase their enmity by attacking, healing or performing specific enmity increasing actions.
Basically Emnity how much attention enemies are giving you.

As a tank you will always want to have every mobs' attention.


Emnity Bar

The Emnity Bar will be one of your closest friends.

The bar will fill up as you attract enemy attention. When your emnity bar has the letter A on it, that means you are the target!
You will need to maintain this emnity level though, as healers' abilities also generate emnity such as the White Mage's Cure.



Generating and Maintaining Emnity

Something that you should know as a tank is generating and maintaining aggro.
Not having aggro on you on all times would result in mobs getting scattered and
Having all mobs attack multiple party members at once which
is a real bad situation for you and your healer

Positioning

Now that you've successfully pulled all these mobs, you now have to position them properly.
Most of the time you need to face the mobs away from your party so when AOEs hit they will be faced away
from the party. When ranged mobs are present, there are certain things to do to make it an efficient pull.

Proper position

This is an example of a proper position when tanking mobs.
Facing the boss away from the group and helping the dps
by giving enough space to hit the flanks and backside

Improper Positions &
Aggro maintenance

This tank does not have aggro on him and as a result the mobs
are all over the place. This is especially dangerous as it could
give the healer a hard time keeping everyone alive.

Summary

So, as a tank, remember that you're job is to lead your party. You need to initiate, create aggro, and maintain aggro.
Position properly and you're group will have an easier time. Pull big if you can but ask your healer if he/she can keep up with you.
Also use your role skills while keeping the cooldown in mind, use them with efficiency in mind.
